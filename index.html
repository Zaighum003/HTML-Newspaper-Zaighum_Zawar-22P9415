QADIANIS DECLARED OUTSIDE THE FOLD OF ISLAM

Home • National News • Constitution • Editorial

According to the Constitution, those who do not believe in the Finality of Prophethood are non-Muslims.

The National Assembly unanimously passes the historic bill amending the constitution. A wave of jubilation across the country. This landmark decision, reached after intense deliberations in the National Assembly, marks a definitive moment in the constitutional history of the nation, aligning legal statutes with the fundamental religious convictions held by the majority of the population.

Assembly Highlights

Key moments from the historic session:

  • Unanimous decision
  • Amendment passed at 5:52 PM
  • Historic constitutional change
  • Crowds gather outside

Bill Details

The bill was presented by the Law Minister and debated for several hours before the final vote was cast.

PM Bhutto: A Solution to a Permanent Problem

ISLAMABAD: The Prime Minister addressed the nation regarding the decision. He stated that this decision reflects the will of the people and the true tenets of the religion.

He emphasized that the protection of the rights of all citizens, including minorities, remains a primary duty of the state. However, the definition of Muslim has now been constitutionally clarified.

"We have solved a 90-year-old problem," the Prime Minister added, noting that the assembly acted with great wisdom and patience throughout the proceedings.

The legal framework will now be adjusted to reflect this amendment, ensuring that the law of the land aligns with the new constitutional reality.

Detainees to be released shortly

In a gesture of national reconciliation, the administration has confirmed that individuals held in custody during the legislative deliberations will be set free. The move is seen as a step towards unifying the nation after the historic parliamentary decision.
